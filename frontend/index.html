Malaysia analyst sentiment — KLCI constituents

What am I looking at?
Each bar shows how professional analysts currently rate one KLCI stock, from Strong Buy (dark green) to Strong Sell (red). The score next to each bar squeezes those ratings into one number from −1 (everyone says sell) to +1 (everyone says buy), with the number of analysts in brackets — more analysts means a more reliable score. The gauge on the right combines every rating on every stock into one overall market mood. Click a stock's name to open its price chart.

KLCI Warrant Gamma Exposure (GEX) — issuer hedging map

What am I looking at?
Banks that issue KLCI warrants must constantly buy and sell FKLI futures to hedge them. This page maps where that hedging is concentrated. Bars grow downward because issuers are always "short gamma" — near those strikes their hedging amplifies market moves instead of damping them. The gamma trough (purple line) marks the index level with the strongest such pressure: expect faster, choppier moves if the KLCI trades into that zone. The readout box combines this with today's market breadth into one practical takeaway.

Malaysia market indexes — FBM index health & per-sector health (%)

What am I looking at?
Index Health % measures how broadly an index is rising: every constituent is checked on four signals — above its moving average, making new highs vs new lows, positive momentum, and RSI — and the results are averaged. +100% means every stock passes every check, −100% means every stock fails; around 0 the market is mixed. The green/red histogram shows how that health has evolved; the line above it is the index itself (an equal-weight proxy, since these indexes have no public price feed). The sector bars underneath show which industries inside the index are driving or dragging it, with the number of member stocks in brackets and the 5-day change.

Risk appetite — FBM ACE / MID 70 / KLCI index spreads (Ziemba turn-of-year & size effect)

What am I looking at?
When investors feel confident they buy risky small companies (FBM ACE) faster than the safe big ones (FBM KLCI); when they get nervous the reverse happens. Each score tracks that gap: above 60 the risky index is outrunning the safe one (risk-on), below 40 money is hiding in large caps (defensive). H_RiskAppetite (black line) is the overall mood. The monthly table tests Ziemba's turn-of-year effect — small caps historically outperforming around Dec–Jan (shaded) — where a t-stat beyond ±2 marks a month whose edge is statistically meaningful, not luck.
